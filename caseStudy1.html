Introduction

Problem: With the growth of the outdoor industry, and the current state of the world, thoughtless use of natural resources is destroying our outdoor spaces more now than ever.

Solution: With the help of information gained from interviews and surveys we developed an application that will allow groups plan efficient and eco-friendly outdoor trips.

My Role: Product Designer, research planning and interviewing, wire-framing, testing, prototype development.

Tools Used: Adobe XD, Figma, Illustrator, Interviews, Surveys, Miro, Trello, InVison, pencil & paper

Skills: interviewing, prototyping, wireframing, illustration, empathy,

Research

Competitive Analysis:
An important first step was to determine who our competition was, where we can offer better services, and where our application would fit in the market. We found a few list-making applications for trip planning, some weather tools, meal planning apps, and hiking/trail map services. However, none of these products did it all.
One particular feature missing in other services was a way to divy up lists to your crew.

The Missing Link:

We needed a way to build a shopping list and divide it amongst the group.

User Interviews:
We developed a list of interview questions to find out how people experience their out-door planning process, what kinds of adventures they go on, and what their frustrations are with getting ready for a campout.

Screening Survey:
To get a broad look at how people camp, we developed a survey and collected data from this as well.

User Persona

Insight Statement:
Annie Jackson, an outdoorsy nurse, needs to help her group coordinate an efficient camping trip because of her preference to be organized, eco-friendly, and prepared for anything.

Problem Statement:
Our camping planning application was designed to help campers plan group adventures. We have observed that existing products don’t make collaborative camping planning easy, which is causing groups to be inefficient, thereby contributing to wasteful use of natural resources. How might we help groups plan for all of their adventure needs and conditions while minimizing environmental impact.

“I hate when everyone brought too much food and we endded up dumping the left-overs.”

Goals:
• Plan efficient group travel, meals, and shopping.
• Keep track of trip details and plans
• Leave-no-trace, relevant earth-friendly habits
